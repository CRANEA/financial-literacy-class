Core Takeaway:

The most important question you can ask any advisor or digital recommendation is: "How are you paid?" Commission-based brokers are incentivized to sell products, whereas legal fiduciaries charge flat advisory fees and put your goals first.

Class Action Checklist:

  • Always verify if a financial planner is a fee-only fiduciary in writing.
  • Disregard speculative investment recommendations on social media.

Core Takeaway:

Many new graduates miscalculate their pay because they mistake gross pay for take-home net pay. Taxes, social security (FICA), and benefits are automatically deducted from your paycheck before the cash lands in your account.

Class Action Checklist:

  • Separate emergency savings from your primary spending account.
  • Build a $1,000 "Financial Firewall" to buffer against unexpected expenses.

Core Takeaway:

Your credit score (FICO) is not a rating of personal worth; it is a mathematical calculation of your default probability. Payment History (35%) and Amounts Owed/Utilization (30%) dictate the score. High-interest credit card debt can easily compound out of control if you make only the minimum payments.

Class Action Checklist:

  • Set up automated calendar alerts or autopay to protect your payment history.
  • Keep credit card balances strictly below 30% of your total credit limit.

Core Takeaway:

Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) plans utilize the "Numerosity Effect" to present small installment amounts (e.g., $150) rather than the true cost ($600). This lowers your cost perception, leading to stacked payments that turn into a silent, cash-depleting subscription.

Class Action Checklist:

  • Avoid using point-of-sale financing for lifestyle or luxury purchases.
  • Analyze any recommendation critically before initiating short-term installment debt.

Core Takeaway:

For young adults, your absolute greatest financial asset is time. Compound interest behaves exponentially over decades. Utilizing a tax-sheltered account like a Roth IRA and investing in broad-market index funds is the safest, most effective way to leverage your time horizon.

Class Action Checklist:

  • Aim to capture any available employer matching contributions as soon as you enter the workforce.
  • Prioritize low-cost index funds over actively managed mutual funds with high hidden fees.
